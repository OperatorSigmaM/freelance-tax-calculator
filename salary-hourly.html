Understanding Salary vs. Hourly Pay

Converting between annual salary and hourly rate helps you compare job offers, negotiate compensation, and understand your true earning potential. Whether you're a freelancer, contractor, or considering a career change, knowing your hourly worth is essential.

Standard Work Calculations

The standard full-time work schedule in the United States is:

  • 40 hours per week
  • 52 weeks per year
  • 2,080 total hours annually (40 √ó 52)

However, many calculations use 50 weeks to account for unpaid vacation time (2 weeks), resulting in 2,000 hours per year.

Salary vs. Hourly: Which is Better?

Salaried positions typically offer:

  • Predictable income
  • Benefits (health insurance, retirement plans)
  • Paid time off
  • Professional development opportunities

Hourly positions typically offer:

  • Overtime pay eligibility
  • Flexible scheduling
  • Clear compensation for extra work
  • Part-time options

Freelance and Contract Rates

When setting freelance rates, remember to account for:

  • Self-employment taxes (15.3% in the US)
  • Health insurance costs
  • Retirement contributions
  • Unpaid time off and business expenses

Many freelancers charge 1.5-2x their target salary rate to cover these additional costs and non-billable time.
